Inpainting models are directly tasked with filling in missing regions of an image. The specific details of CTSDG (Conditional Transformer for Structural Degradation) suggest an ability to handle structural degradation, which is key for maintaining sharp contours and aligning structure.

[cite_start]It uses a learning-based approach as a binary classification task on image pairs, taking the spatial distribution of local keypoints and matches as input to reason about both local and global cues[cite: 36, 38]. While not a direct inpainting model, its ability to understand and differentiate complex 3D structures and their visual similarities suggests a strong underlying capability to interpret and maintain structural coherence during image completion. This understanding of "similar structures" could help guide the inpainting process to create structurally consistent hair and background.

While primarily for multimodal object detection, its ability to "paste objects" with multimodal consistency implies a strong understanding of how visual elements (textures, shapes) integrate into a scene, which could be leveraged to ensure smooth textures and aligned contours when filling in missing parts. [cite_start]The re-sampling of point clouds specifically highlights its awareness of 3D structure[cite: 44].
